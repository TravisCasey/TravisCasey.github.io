N-player games with reinforcement learning

An exploration into the extension of canonical and modern game-playing techniques to a wider class of games, particularly those with more than two players. Includes games and agents implemented in Python, with a deep Q-learning agent implemented in PyTorch.
A work in progress.

Pound-Drever-Hall technique with FPGA

Replacements for dedicated signal processing and control instruments are implemented on a combination FPGA and microprocessor board. The key systems are the lock-in amplifier and the PID controller, which are defined in Verilog.
Developed with the OU Center for Quantum Research and Technology.
